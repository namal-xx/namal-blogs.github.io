How I got here

No classroom. No degree.
Just building.

I'm a machine learning engineer who got here by building things, not collecting certificates. Computer vision, CNNs, real client projects — that's what taught me ML, not a classroom.

I started by being obsessed with one question: how do machines learn to see? That curiosity turned into experiments, experiments turned into projects, and projects turned into a deployed CRM intelligence system used by real clients.

Outside the code

F1, strategy, and
why it all connects.

When I'm not training models or pushing PRs, I'm watching F1 and thinking about what race strategy and machine learning have in common — more than people realise.

Both are about making the best decision possible with imperfect data, under time pressure, where the cost of a bad call compounds fast. That mindset shapes how I think about ML problems.
